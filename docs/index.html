DENV Full-Genome Dataset — Availability Report

CNRS UMR2K Seed Grant 2026  ·  Author: Camila Duitama  ·  Generated: 10 Jul 2026

Full-genome criteria applied throughout this report:
length ≥ 10,000 bp  AND  genome_coverage ≥ 0.95  AND  is_lab_host ≠ True (512 lab-adapted sequences removed)  AND  host is Human or Mosquito.
Other host categories (primates, rodents) and sequences with no host annotation are excluded. Data source: Nextstrain DENV (nextstrain.org/dengue, updated 2026-07-02), which curates sequences from GenBank and adds Nextclade clade/lineage annotations and per-gene coverage metrics.
Total sequences in this report: 15,072 (15,002 human, 70 mosquito).

Figure 2 — Sequence length distribution — all Nextstrain sequences

Histogram of all sequences before quality filtering. The red dashed line marks the 10,000 bp length threshold. Note the bimodal distribution: short amplicons cluster below 2 kb; near-complete and complete genomes cluster above 9 kb. Sequences to the right of the cutoff are further filtered by genome coverage (Figure 3).

Figure 3 — Genome coverage distribution — sequences ≥10 kb (all hosts)

Distribution of genome_coverage (fraction of the reference genome covered by the alignment) for all Nextstrain sequences passing the length filter, regardless of host. Shown here for context: the red dashed line at 0.95 is the threshold applied in this report. Length alone is insufficient: a sequence can be ≥10 kb yet have low coverage due to amplicon tiling or assembly gaps. Only sequences to the right of the red line (and with human or mosquito host) are included in all other figures and tables.

Figure 4 — Mean per-gene coverage across full genomes (mean ± SD)

Mean coverage per gene across all human and mosquito full genomes, with error bars showing ±1 SD. Values close to 1.0 indicate that the gene is fully covered. The yellow band highlights NS1 — the primary study target gene. Low mean or high SD for a gene may indicate incomplete assemblies in a subset of sequences.

Collection year distribution of full genomes by serotype. Sequences with missing or unparseable collection dates are excluded. This shows the historical sampling depth available for phylogenetic analyses.
