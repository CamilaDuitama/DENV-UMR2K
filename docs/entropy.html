Pipeline

H in bits (0 = conserved → 4.32 = maximally diverse). Hstd = H / log₂20 (0–1). Analysed independently per serotype × host. Min 5 sequences per group after clustering. Approach adapted from Testa et al. 2026 (Nat Ecol Evol). Key difference from Testa et al.: we align at the amino acid level (MAFFT --amino) and compute entropy directly, skipping the RevTrans step (back-translation to a codon alignment). RevTrans is only needed for phylogenetic codon models (phydms, future WP2) and is not required for amino acid entropy.

Figure 1 — Mean per-gene Shannon entropy by serotype and host

Mean Shannon entropy H (bits) per gene, split by serotype and host. Dotted line = genome-wide mean for human sequences (per serotype). Dashed line = genome-wide mean for mosquito sequences (per serotype). Yellow bands = NS4A–2K–NS4B target region.

Key findingMost variable genes (human): prM (1.14 bits), E (1.10), NS2A (1.10), NS4B (1.08) — genes under direct antibody or immune selection. Most conserved: 2K (0.93), NS2B (1.02), NS4A (1.02). Note: all genes cluster within a narrow range (0.93–1.14 bits); differences are real but modest. Target region (NS4A–2K–NS4B) mean = 1.01 bits, slightly below the genome-wide mean (1.06 bits).
Figure 2 — Entropy heatmap across the full proteome

Mean H per 20-site sliding window across the DENV polyprotein (all serotype × host combinations). Redder = more variable; bluer = more conserved. Yellow bands = target region (NS4A–2K–NS4B).

Key findingDENV2 is notably more conserved (0.77 bits) than DENV1/3/4 (1.1–1.2 bits) — visible as the consistently blue row across all panels. The target region shows a relatively conserved band across serotypes.
Figure 3 — Mean entropy in the target region (NS4A–2K–NS4B) per serotype

Mean H ± SD per gene in the NS4A–2K–NS4B target region (human sequences only). Serotype groups with fewer than 10 informative sequences are excluded. Dashed reference lines = genome-wide mean H per serotype.

Key findingConservation within the target region varies by serotype: in DENV1/3/4, 2K (signal peptide) is the most conserved gene (lowest H). In DENV2, NS4A and NS4B are more conserved than 2K (0.80 and 0.81 vs 1.04 bits). DENV2 is the most conserved serotype across all three target genes — making it the best reference for DMS experimental design. Only human sequences shown (mosquito N < 10 after clustering).
Figure 4 — Human vs mosquito entropy — rank correlation

Each point is the gene-level mean entropy (H) for one serotype × gene combination (DENV1/2/4 only; DENV3 mosquito excluded). Gene-level means pool hundreds of amino acid sites per gene. Dotted diagonal = perfect agreement. Dashed line = linear regression. ⚠️ Mosquito N = 5–9 sequences per serotype group after clustering.

Key findingThe rank order of conservation is consistent between hosts (Pearson r = 0.77): genes variable in humans tend to be variable in mosquitoes. However, mosquito entropy values are systematically lower than human values across all genes. This offset cannot be attributed to biology with the current data: Shannon entropy is biased downward at small N (mosquito N = 5–9 vs human N = 176–620), which alone could explain the gap. Larger mosquito datasets would be needed to separate sampling bias from real host differences. This figure should be treated as exploratory.
