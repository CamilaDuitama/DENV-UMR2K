Pipeline

H in bits (0 = conserved → 4.32 = maximally diverse). Hstd = H / log₂20 (0–1). All figures use Miller–Madow bias-corrected entropy (HMM = H + (K−1)/2N), which reduces underestimation bias from small sample sizes. Analysed independently per serotype × host. Min 5 sequences per group after clustering. Approach adapted from Testa et al. 2026 (Nat Ecol Evol). Key difference from Testa et al.: we align at the amino acid level (MAFFT --amino) and compute entropy directly, skipping the RevTrans step (back-translation to a codon alignment). RevTrans is only needed for phylogenetic codon models (phydms, future WP2) and is not required for amino acid entropy.

Figure 1 — Mean per-gene Shannon entropy by serotype and host

Mean Shannon entropy H (bits) per gene, split by serotype and host. Dotted line = gene-unweighted mean Hₘₘ (MM-corrected, bits) for human sequences (per serotype). Dashed line = gene-unweighted mean Hₘₘ (MM-corrected, bits) for mosquito sequences (per serotype). Yellow bands = NS4A–2K–NS4B target region.

Key findingMost variable genes (human): prM (1.14), E (1.11), NS2A (1.10), NS1 (1.08) — genes under antibody or immune selection pressure. Most conserved: 2K (0.99), NS2B (1.02), NS4A (1.02). All genes span 0.99–1.14 bits (Hₘₘ (MM-corrected, bits)). Target region mean = 1.03 bits vs genome mean 1.06 bits.
Figure 2 — Entropy heatmap across the full proteome

Mean H per 20-site sliding window across the DENV polyprotein (all serotype × host combinations). Redder = more variable; bluer = more conserved. Yellow bands = target region (NS4A–2K–NS4B).

Key findingDENV2 is notably more conserved (0.77 bits) than DENV1/3/4 (1.1–1.2 bits) — visible as the consistently blue row across all panels. The target region shows a relatively conserved band across serotypes.
Figure 3 — Mean entropy in the target region (NS4A–2K–NS4B) per serotype

Mean H ± SD per gene in the NS4A–2K–NS4B target region (human sequences only). Serotype groups with fewer than 10 informative sequences are excluded. Dashed reference lines = genome-wide mean H per serotype.

Key findingConservation within the target region varies by serotype. In DENV1/3/4, 2K is most conserved (DENV1=1.070, DENV3=1.015, DENV4=0.846 bits). In DENV2 only, NS4A and NS4B are more conserved than 2K (0.800 and 0.817 vs 1.043 bits). DENV2 is the most conserved serotype for NS4A and NS4B. For 2K, the most conserved serotype is DENV4 (0.846 bits). Values: Hₘₘ (MM-corrected, bits). Human sequences only (mosquito N < 10 after clustering).
